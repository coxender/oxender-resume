About Me

As someone who grew up in the rural Midwest, I never considered computer science or software developement as a possible job, let alone a job I was capable of doing. I didn't fins the career path until my second year of college, when I took my first coding class. Immediately, I went from not being interested in coding, to sitting in office hours every chance I could, asking my somewhat exasperated professor how to do this and that (even then, she was the person who inspired me to get where I am).

Three years later, I got the chance to dive into STEM, I changed my major, and learned as many skills as I could. Whether it was a chance to hold calculus teaching session and tutor new college students, to moderating servers and writing code to manage them, to the in's and out's of cloud cybersecurity at a Fortune 500 company, I have been blessed to try an array of opportunities. Now that I am a few short months from graduating with my bachelors, I am so excited to see what lies ahead!

Career Aspirations

Although I have more experience in cybersecurity and machine learning, the career that I aspire to is a DevOps Engineering. In my long term plan, I have been developing my front-end skills and my bash skills, while planning to learn a fuller knowledge of the software and skills needed for this job in the coming months.
